Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a license to refer business loans in Canada?
No. Business loan referral and brokerage in Canada does not require a specific license the way mortgage brokering does. You can refer clients and earn fees without regulatory licensing requirements, though you should operate transparently and professionally in all client dealings.
How much can I earn as a business loan referral partner?
Referral fees on funded business loans typically range from a flat fee per deal to a percentage of the origination fee. The amount depends on deal size, product type, and your level of involvement. Abria discusses compensation transparently before any agreement — no surprises.
What is the difference between a referral partner and a sub-broker?
A referral partner introduces a client and steps back — Abria handles everything from assessment through funding. A sub-broker stays more involved, developing the client relationship and working alongside Abria through the process. Sub-brokers typically earn more per deal because they contribute more to it.
Do I need experience in finance to become a referral partner?
No. Referral partners don't need finance experience — they need trusted relationships with business owners. The financing expertise sits with Abria. What you bring is the relationship and the introduction. For sub-brokers who want to be more involved, Abria provides guidance on deal assessment and product matching.
Is there a minimum deal size or volume requirement?
No minimum volume to start. We evaluate each deal on its own merits. The right first step is a conversation about whether your client's situation is something we can realistically help with — not a commitment to bring a certain number of deals per month.
How does Abria protect my client relationship?
Your client is your client. Abria does not market directly to clients introduced by partners, does not cross-sell unrelated services, and keeps you informed on deal progress. The handoff is a professional collaboration — not a transfer of the relationship.
