Business Loans in Canada: Term Financing for Growth, Acquisition, and Operations

A business loan provides a fixed amount of capital repaid over a defined term. It's the most common form of business financing in Canada — and one where the quality of the application often determines the outcome more than the underlying strength of the business.

How Business Loans Work in Canada

A term business loan advances a lump sum repaid over 1 to 10 years depending on the purpose, lender type, and security available. Interest rates range from prime plus 1-2% at chartered banks to 12-30%+ at alternative lenders. Banks require comprehensive documentation and take 2-6 weeks. Alternative lenders require less, move in 24-72 hours, and assess risk primarily on revenue and time in business. The right lender depends on your profile — not just who has the best rate.

What Canadian Lenders Evaluate

Every lender — bank, credit union, or alternative — asks the same core questions: Can this business repay the loan? Does the owner understand their own financials? Is the risk manageable given what's been presented? The six factors carrying the most weight are: time in business, monthly revenue consistency, personal and business credit profile, use-of-funds specificity, existing debt obligations, and the quality of the application itself. A disorganized, incomplete application raises doubt about management competence — often enough to tip a borderline decision toward decline.

Business Loan Amounts Available in Canada

Business loan amounts range from $10,000 at alternative lenders to several million at chartered banks. The amount available is primarily determined by monthly revenue, time in business, existing debt load, and the purpose of the funds. Most alternative lenders advance between 50% and 150% of average monthly revenue for unsecured loans. Equipment-secured and real-estate-secured loans can access larger facilities because the asset reduces lender risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get a business loan in Canada?
Apply with a lender suited to your profile. Prepare 6 months of bank statements, financial statements, and a specific use-of-funds explanation. Working with a capital advisory firm to prepare the application before submission significantly improves approval odds.
What credit score do I need for a business loan in Canada?
Chartered banks typically require 650+. Alternative lenders often work with 550-620, placing more weight on monthly revenue. Equipment financing lenders may be more flexible because the asset serves as collateral.
How long does it take to get a business loan in Canada?
Alternative lenders can fund in 24-72 hours. Banks typically take 2-6 weeks. Speed depends significantly on how complete your application is at submission.
What is the minimum revenue to qualify for a business loan in Canada?
Most alternative lenders require $10,000-$15,000 in average monthly revenue. Banks typically require considerably more. Revenue consistency matters as much as the average.
Can a startup get a business loan in Canada?
Yes, though startups need a strong business plan, personal credit in good standing, and often a personal guarantee. Government-backed programs like the CSBFP can help startups access financing for eligible assets.
