The Reality

The Reality Most Franchise Owners Face

You bought a system. The numbers in the disclosure document looked reasonable. The franchisor's support was supposed to be there. But the reality of running a franchise often looks very different from what was sold — and when things go wrong, you can feel completely alone inside a structure designed by the other side.

Franchise agreements are written to protect the franchisor. The royalty structure, territory clauses, renewal terms, and exit provisions were all drafted by their lawyers. Most franchisees sign them without fully understanding what they are agreeing to.

We are not franchise salespeople. We are independent advisors with no relationship with any franchisor and no incentive other than giving you an accurate picture of your situation.

Situations We Help With

Underperforming Franchise

Revenue is below projections and you cannot identify why. We assess your operations, your location, your staffing, and your franchisor relationship — and give you an honest outside view of what is driving the underperformance and what can realistically change it.

Before you sign anything, an independent review of the opportunity — the FDD, the territory, the unit economics, and the franchisor's track record — can save you from a very expensive mistake.

Franchisor Relationship Problems

Disputes with your franchisor — over territory, support, performance standards, or renewal — require experienced navigation. We help you understand your position and what options are available to you.

Franchise Exit Strategy

Whether you want to sell, transfer, or terminate — franchise exits are complex. We help you plan an exit that maximizes what you walk away with.

Multi-Unit Expansion

You are profitable and considering adding locations. We help you assess whether the unit economics actually support expansion and whether your operations are ready to scale.
