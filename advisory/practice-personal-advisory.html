Separation & Divorce Financial Advisory

Financial clarity, practical organization, and experienced guidance through separation, divorce, and major life transitions — before the legal process consumes you.

Major personal transitions — separation, divorce, the shift from employment to self-employment — carry a level of financial and legal complexity that most people navigate with a combination of expensive professional counsel and incomplete information. The result is often poor decisions made under pressure, at significant personal and financial cost.

Our personal advisory practice exists to change that equation. We provide experienced, candid guidance that helps individuals understand their situation clearly — often before engaging legal counsel, which means they enter those conversations better prepared, ask better questions, and make more informed decisions.

Separation & Divorce for the Self-Employed

Separation and divorce are complex for anyone. For self-employed individuals and business owners, they are significantly more so. Income is often variable, irregular, or structured in ways that are difficult to interpret through a standard financial lens. Business assets, corporate structures, and shareholder agreements introduce layers of complexity that require someone who understands how businesses actually work — not just how they appear on paper.

Our team has worked extensively alongside lawyers, accountants, and mediators navigating these processes on behalf of clients — giving us a practical, grounded understanding of how separations and divorces actually unfold for self-employed individuals and business owners. We understand what the numbers mean, how income is structured, what assets look like from both sides of the table, and where the most significant decisions are made — and where the most costly mistakes occur.

We work alongside legal and financial professionals — not as a replacement for them, but as an experienced guide who helps clients enter those relationships better prepared, ask the right questions, and avoid the costly mistakes that come from navigating complex processes without context.
