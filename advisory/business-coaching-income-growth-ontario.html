What We Work On

The Real Drivers of Income Growth

Revenue Model & Pricing

Are you charging what you are worth? Most self-employed professionals are underpriced — and the gap between what they charge and what the market will pay is often the single biggest income lever available to them.

Sales Process & Pipeline

How are you finding clients and converting them? A clear, consistent sales process is the difference between a practice that grows predictably and one that lives in feast-and-famine cycles.

Focus & Time Allocation

What are you spending your time on versus what actually drives income? Most self-employed professionals spend significant time on low-value activity while underinvesting in the things that actually move the needle.

Industry & Opportunity Mapping

Not sure what you could earn in a particular industry or role? We have deep experience across financial services, real estate, automotive, technology, and more — and can give you a realistic picture of what the income ceiling looks like and what it takes to reach it.

Accountability & Momentum

Knowing what to do and actually doing it are two different things. A monthly advisory relationship keeps you accountable, challenges you when you are drifting, and ensures the plans you make actually get executed.

Common Questions

Questions We Hear Most Often

I am self-employed and want to make more money. Where do I even start? +

Start with a free discovery call. We will spend 30 minutes understanding your situation — what you do, what you earn, what you want to earn, and what you think is in the way. In most cases we can identify two or three specific, actionable changes within that first conversation. There is no charge and no obligation.

How much can I realistically expect to earn in my industry? +

This is one of the most common questions we get — and one of our most valuable conversations. Our team has direct operating experience across insurance brokerage, mortgage brokerage, real estate, financial services, automotive, and technology. We can give you a realistic picture of what top performers in your industry earn, what separates them from average performers, and what it practically takes to get there. That conversation alone is often worth the cost of months of advisory.

Is business coaching worth the money? +

It depends entirely on the quality of the coaching and whether it actually changes what you do. Generic coaching programs with frameworks and worksheets rarely move the needle. What works is direct, experienced guidance from someone who has built businesses and generated real income in your industry — and who holds you accountable to the changes you identify. Our clients consistently find that a single improved conversation, a pricing correction, or a new approach to their sales process returns the cost of months of advisory in a single transaction.

I am a real estate agent / insurance broker / mortgage agent — can you help me specifically? +

Yes — and we have deep specific experience in each of those industries. Our team has built and managed brokerages, recruited and trained agents, and generated significant volume across insurance and mortgage. We understand the specific sales cycles, the income models, the licensing environment, and the difference between practitioners who build sustainable high-income practices and those who don't. We speak your language.

I am considering a career change. Can you help me figure out what I could earn in a new industry? +

Absolutely. Career and industry mapping is one of the most valuable conversations we have. We can give you a frank, experience-based view of what different industries and roles realistically pay at different levels, what it takes to get there, and how your existing skills and experience translate. This is the kind of honest, informed perspective that most people can only get from someone who has been deep inside the industries they are considering.

How is this different from a business coach I find online? +

Most business coaches are trained in coaching methodologies — they are skilled at asking questions and holding space for your thinking. What they typically lack is direct industry operating experience. Our advisors have built businesses, managed teams, generated significant personal and corporate income, and navigated the specific challenges that self-employed professionals face. We do not just ask good questions — we give you direct, experienced answers based on having done what you are trying to do.
