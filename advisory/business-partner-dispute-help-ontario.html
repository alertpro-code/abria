The Situation

What Is Actually at Stake

Most business partnerships start with trust and shared vision. They break down over money, decision-making, unequal contribution, strategic disagreements, or simply the passage of time. When they do, both partners face an impossible situation: the business they built together now has two owners who cannot work together — and every day the conflict continues costs money, energy, and often customers.

The instinct is to call a lawyer. And you may well need one. But before the legal process begins, there is a critical window where experienced, business-focused advisory can save significant time, money, and sometimes the partnership itself.

We have owned equity and navigated the complexity of shared ownership. We understand how these situations actually play out — and we can help you think through your options clearly before emotions and legal costs narrow them.

Where We Help

Situations We Help With

Partner Dispute Mediation Prep

Before you enter mediation or negotiation with your partner, you need a clear picture of your position — your ownership rights, your contribution to the business, and what a fair resolution actually looks like. We help you arrive prepared.

Shareholder Buyout Negotiation

Whether you are buying out a partner or being bought out, valuation and structure matter enormously. We help you understand what the business is worth, how buyouts are typically structured, and how to negotiate from an informed position.

Deadlocked Decision-Making

50/50 splits without dispute resolution mechanisms are the most common structure — and the most dangerous when partners disagree. We help deadlocked owners explore exit paths before the business deteriorates further.

Unequal Contribution Disputes

One partner is working. The other is not. The equity split no longer reflects the reality of who is running the business. We help owners document their position and explore restructuring options that reflect the current reality.

Post-Dispute Business Stabilization

After a partnership ends — however it ends — the business needs to stabilize and move forward. We help the remaining owners rebuild structure, reset direction, and protect what they have worked to build.

Before Legal Costs Take Over

Before Legal Costs Consume the Equity

Shareholder and partner disputes that reach litigation in Ontario can cost each party $50,000–$200,000 or more in legal fees — often consuming the very equity they are fighting over. Many disputes that reach litigation could have been resolved earlier with structured, business-focused advisory before positions hardened.

We are not mediators and we are not lawyers. But we can help you understand your situation clearly, think through your options strategically, and arrive at any professional process in a far stronger position.
