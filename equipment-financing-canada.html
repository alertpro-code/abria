Equipment Financing in Canada: Finance What Your Business Runs On

Equipment financing lets Canadian businesses acquire the machinery, vehicles, and tools they need without depleting working capital. The equipment itself serves as security — which means qualification criteria are often more accessible than unsecured lending.

Why Equipment Financing Works Differently

In a standard unsecured business loan, the lender's protection is your creditworthiness and cash flow. In equipment financing, the equipment itself is collateral. The lender can recover the asset if payments stop — which fundamentally changes the risk calculation in your favour.

This is why equipment financing typically offers lower rates than unsecured lending, longer repayment terms, and more flexible qualification criteria. A business that doesn't qualify for a $150,000 unsecured working capital loan might qualify readily for a $150,000 equipment facility, because the lender has a recoverable asset backing the advance.

Equipment Loan vs. Equipment Lease

Equipment loans transfer ownership immediately. You finance the purchase price over 2-7 years and own the equipment outright at term end. Interest is tax-deductible and you can claim capital cost allowance (CCA/depreciation) on the asset. Best for equipment with long useful lives you intend to own permanently.

Equipment leases keep ownership with the lender — you pay to use the equipment and typically have a purchase option at term end. Lease payments may be fully deductible as operating expenses. Better suited for technology or equipment that becomes obsolete quickly, or when preserving balance sheet flexibility matters.

What You Need to Apply

Equipment financing applications are simpler than standard business loans. You'll typically need: 3-6 months of business bank statements, a quote or invoice for the equipment being financed, basic business information (registration, ownership, time in operation), and personal ID for owners. The equipment quote is important — lenders want to confirm the asset's value and condition.

New vs. Used Equipment Financing

Both new and used equipment can be financed in Canada. Used equipment financing is slightly more complex because lenders evaluate the asset's age, condition, and marketability in addition to your business profile. Equipment that holds value well — heavy machinery, commercial vehicles, medical devices — is straightforward to finance even used. Highly specialized or rapidly depreciating equipment requires stronger credit support.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does equipment financing work in Canada?
Equipment financing allows businesses to acquire machinery, vehicles, or commercial equipment by spreading the cost over 2 to 7 years. The equipment itself serves as collateral, making approval more accessible than unsecured lending and typically resulting in lower rates.
What equipment can be financed in Canada?
Most commercial equipment with identifiable resale value — construction machinery, commercial vehicles, restaurant equipment, medical devices, manufacturing machinery, and IT hardware. The equipment must have commercial value that can serve as security.
Can I get equipment financing with bad credit in Canada?
Yes, in many cases. Because the equipment serves as collateral, lenders are often willing to work with lower credit scores. Strong monthly revenue and a clear business need for the equipment help offset credit concerns significantly.
Is it better to lease or buy equipment in Canada?
It depends on the equipment type, your tax situation, and whether long-term ownership is the goal. Loans make more sense for equipment with long useful lives you want to own. Leases suit technology or equipment that becomes obsolete quickly. The tax treatment of each differs — worth discussing with your accountant.
