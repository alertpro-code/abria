Bad Credit Business Loans in Canada: Your Options, Explained

Bad credit limits your options — but it doesn't eliminate them. The key is knowing which lenders look at what, and making sure the parts of your application you can control are as strong as possible.

Can You Get a Business Loan With Bad Credit in Canada?

Yes — and more commonly than most business owners expect. A credit score below 600 will close most bank doors, but alternative lenders, equipment financing companies, invoice lenders, and private capital sources operate on different criteria entirely. Many of them weight monthly revenue and time in business far more heavily than personal credit score.

The honest answer is that bad credit raises your cost of capital and narrows your lender options — but a business with consistent monthly revenue, a specific funding need, and a well-prepared application can access financing in Canada even with a challenging credit profile.

What Lenders Actually Look at When Credit Is a Challenge

Monthly revenue and consistency. Most alternative lenders require $10,000-$15,000 in average monthly deposits as a minimum. Revenue consistency matters as much as the average — stable, predictable deposits from a recognizable pattern of business activity carry more weight than a high average with significant variability.

Time in business. Three to six months of operating history opens doors with many alternative lenders. Longer history (12+ months) opens more. A business with 2+ years of consistent revenue can often access financing with a credit score in the 580-620 range that a bank would never touch.

Use of funds specificity. A specific, credible use-of-funds explanation reduces lender hesitation regardless of credit profile. "Working capital" tells a lender nothing. "$35,000 to purchase a used excavator to complete two signed contracts" tells them exactly what the money does and how the business will repay it.

The application itself. A complete, organized, professionally presented file signals competence — which matters more than people realize when credit is already a concern. An incomplete or disorganized file compounds doubt. A complete file reduces it.

Financing Options That Work With Bad Credit in Canada

🏗️

Equipment Financing

The most accessible bad credit option. The equipment serves as collateral — shifting the lender's risk away from your credit score and toward the asset's value. Many equipment lenders work with scores in the 560-600 range.

💰

Alternative Working Capital

Revenue-based assessment — monthly deposits and time in business carry most of the weight. Credit score is a factor but rarely the deciding one. Approvals in 24-72 hours. Higher cost than bank financing.

📄

Invoice Financing

Your customers' creditworthiness matters more than yours. If your business carries significant receivables with reliable commercial clients, invoice financing may be accessible regardless of your credit profile.

🔒

Secured Financing

Offering security — equipment, inventory, receivables, or real estate — changes the lender's risk calculation. Secured financing is almost always more accessible than unsecured, especially when credit is a concern.

What NOT to Do When You Have Bad Credit and Need Financing

Don't apply broadly to multiple lenders at once. Every hard credit inquiry reduces your score slightly. Multiple inquiries in a short window signal potential distress to subsequent lenders — compounding the credit problem you're trying to overcome. Apply strategically to one well-matched lender with a strong application.

Don't conceal CRA debt. Outstanding tax obligations are discovered during underwriting. Disclosure upfront — with a payment arrangement in place — is far better than the lender finding it themselves. Undisclosed CRA debt is an almost certain decline.

Don't submit a vague application. When credit is already a concern, every other part of your application needs to be stronger — not weaker. A specific use-of-funds narrative, clean bank statements, and an organized file reduce hesitation. A disorganized application confirms it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a business loan with bad credit in Canada?
Yes. Alternative lenders, equipment finance companies, and invoice lenders evaluate applications primarily on monthly revenue, time in business, and cash flow — not credit score alone. A business with consistent revenue and a clear funding need can often access financing with a personal credit score below 600.
What is the easiest business loan to get with bad credit in Canada?
Equipment financing is often the most accessible because the equipment serves as collateral. Short-term working capital loans from alternative lenders are also accessible — assessed primarily on monthly revenue. Invoice financing evaluates your customers' creditworthiness more than yours.
What credit score do I need for a small business loan in Canada?
Banks typically require 650+. Alternative lenders often work with 550-620. Equipment lenders may accept lower scores. The minimum depends on the lender type and how strong other factors — revenue, time in business, use of funds — are in your application.
Can I get a startup business loan with bad credit in Canada?
Startups with bad credit face a compounded challenge — limited history and a weak credit profile together. Equipment financing against a specific asset, or government-backed programs like the CSBFP with a strong business plan, are the most viable paths. Abria assesses startup situations case by case.
Does applying for a business loan hurt my credit score in Canada?
The hard credit inquiry from a loan application typically reduces your score by 5-10 points temporarily. Multiple applications in a short window compound this. Apply strategically to one well-matched lender rather than broadly to several at once.
